--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 PATTERNS IN MISSES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. DECISIONS DOMINATE MISSES
   12 of 16 misses ended in Unanimous Decision; 1 in Split Decision.
   13/16 (81%) were decisions vs 3/16 (19%) finishes.
   This is a structural limitation of the model: statistical differentials are
   well-suited for predicting WHO HAS THE EDGE but poorly suited for the marginal
   elite-vs-elite matchups that go to the scorecards. The fighters in title bouts
   are the most evenly matched in the division, so small stat differences carry
   less predictive weight.

2. HIGH-CONFIDENCE MISSES STAND OUT
   Two misses exceeded 60% confidence — these are the model's biggest blind spots:

   - UFC 324 Gaethje vs. Pimblett (61.2% on Pimblett):
     Gaethje's career stats (high output, durable) came in well below Pimblett's
     recent rolling metrics. But Gaethje's experience in elite wrestling/grappling
     situations — which the model partly captures but not fully — proved decisive.

   - UFC 323 Dvalishvili vs. Yan 2 (60.4% on Dvalishvili):
     Dvalishvili held the title with a long win streak. Yan had gone 0-3 from Apr 2022
     to Mar 2023 (losses to Sterling, O'Malley, Dvalishvili), then won 3 straight
     (Yadong Mar 2024, Figueiredo Nov 2024, McGhee Jul 2025) before UFC 323. Those
     3 losses likely depressed his rolling metrics enough to make the model underrate
     his skill ceiling despite his active win streak entering the fight.

   Notable non-misses at high confidence (model got these right despite difficulty):
   - UFC 302 Makhachev vs. Poirier (64.6%): Model correctly backed Makhachev.
   - UFC 309 Jones vs. Miocic (62.6%): Model correctly backed Jones despite ~3-year layoff (Feb 2020 – Mar 2023).

3. CHAMPION DEFENSE IS INCONSISTENTLY HANDLED
   Several misses involved reigning champions losing to challengers who had better
   recent form: Edwards→Muhammad, Du Plessis→Chimaev (Chimaev won), Dvalishvili→Yan.
   The model has no explicit "championship experience" or "pressure performance"
   variable. It treats each fighter as a statistical profile, which can disadvantage
   champions who have pulled back activity or face stylistically difficult opponents.

4. LOSING STREAK DISTORTION
   Yan went 0-3 from Apr 2022 to Mar 2023 (Sterling, O'Malley, Dvalishvili), then
   won 3 straight before UFC 323. Those 3 consecutive losses depressed his rolling
   metrics even though he entered the rematch on a win streak — the model backed
   Dvalishvili (60.4%) but Yan won. Jones (~3-year layoff, Feb 2020 – Mar 2023) is
   a contrast case where genuine inactivity existed but career differential features
   were sufficient to identify him. Volkanovski (post-KO-loss): model incorrectly
   backed Lopes twice (#26, #42), undervaluing Volkanovski's historical profile.
   A future feature: explicit "recent loss weight decay" or win-streak recency bias.

5. GENDER DIVISION PARITY
   Women's title fights: 6 correct / 10 total = 60.0% — essentially matching the
   male title fight rate (20/32 = 62.5%). The model performs evenly across both.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 NOTABLE CORRECT CALLS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 - UFC 298 Topuria vs. Volkanovski (60.5%): Model backed the challenger Topuria —
   correctly identifying his superior recent form and striking output vs. a
   Volkanovski who had been KO'd recently.

 - UFC 309 Jones vs. Miocic (62.6%): Model correctly backed Jones despite his
   ~3-year layoff (Feb 2020 – Mar 2023). Rolling metrics for active fighters
   (Miocic) looked stronger, but Jones's career differential features were
   sufficient to identify him.

 - UFC 317 Topuria vs. Oliveira (67.0%): Highest confidence correct pick in the
   title subset. Topuria's dominant output differentials were the strongest signal.

 - UFC 302 / UFC 311 Makhachev bouts: Model was 2/2 — correctly picked Makhachev
   over Poirier (64.6%) and over Moicano (64.5%). High confidence, both correct.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 TAKEAWAYS FOR FUTURE MODEL IMPROVEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 1. Add "inactivity penalty" feature — days since last fight weighted by context
    (injury layoff vs. deliberate rest vs. quick turnaround).

 2. Style matchup encoding — current features are symmetric differentials. A
    grappler-vs-grappler vs. grappler-vs-striker interaction term could improve
    title fight prediction where style matchup is decisive.

 3. Champion momentum — binary flag for "defending champion in title fight" or
    a "consecutive title defenses" count, capturing pressure-performance dynamics.

 4. Per-round analysis — if the model was fed round-by-round pacing profiles
    (from the endurance dashboard feature), it could better handle marathon
    specialists vs. early-finisher profiles in 5-round fights.
